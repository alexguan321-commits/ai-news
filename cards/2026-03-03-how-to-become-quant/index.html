How I'd Become a Quant If I Had to Start Over Tomorrow

Author: gemchanger (@gemchange_ltd) Date: March 3, 2026 Engagement: 12,320 likes | 1,360 reposts | 183 replies | 8.23M views

Key Data Points

  • Entry-level quant compensation (2025): $300K-$500K total comp at top firms
  • AI/ML hiring in finance: 88% year-over-year growth
  • Jane Street average employee compensation: $1.4M/year (H1 2025)
  • Learning timeline: ~18 months from zero to competent

The 5-Level Learning Path

Part I: Probability (3-4 weeks)

  • Conditional thinking: P(A|B) = P(A∩B) / P(B)
  • Bayes' theorem for real-time belief updating
  • Expected value and variance
  • Homework: Blitzstein & Hwang (Harvard), simulate 10K coin flips, implement Bayesian updater

Part II: Statistics (4-5 weeks)

  • Hypothesis testing as BS detector
  • Multiple comparisons problem (test 1,000 strategies → 50 show p<0.05 by chance)
  • Regression: α (alpha) is your edge, everything else is market exposure
  • Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)
  • Homework: Wasserman "All of Statistics", Fama-French 3-factor regression, permutation tests

Part III: Linear Algebra (4-6 weeks)

  • Covariance matrices: 500 stocks → 500×500 matrix with 125,250 entries
  • Eigendecomposition: first 5 eigenvectors explain 70% of variance in 500-stock universe
  • Markowitz portfolio theory: portfolio variance = quadratic form
  • Homework: Gilbert Strang MIT 18.06, PCA on S&P 500, mean-variance optimization

Part IV: Calculus & Optimization (4-5 weeks)

  • Taylor expansion: delta hedging (1st order), gamma hedging (2nd order)
  • Itô calculus: (dW_t)² = dt is why stochastic calculus differs from ordinary calculus
  • Homework: Boyd & Vandenberghe "Convex Optimization", gradient descent, cvxpy portfolio optimization

Part V: Stochastic Calculus (6-8 weeks - hardest level)

  • Brownian motion: dW_t has "size" √dt, so (dW_t)² = dt
  • Itô's lemma: df = f'(x)dx + ½f''(x)(dW_t)²
  • Black-Scholes derivation from delta-hedging
  • The Greeks: Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho
  • Homework: Shreve "Stochastic Calculus for Finance II", derive Black-Scholes from scratch

Polymarket & LMSR

Logarithmic Market Scoring Rule (LMSR) by Robin Hanson:

  • Cost function: C(q) = b * ln(Σ exp(q_i/b))
  • Price = softmax function (same as neural network classifiers)
  • Market maker's max loss bounded at b * ln(n)

Career Landscape

4 Archetypes:

1. Quant Researcher - PhD-level, finds patterns, builds models 2. Quant Developer - Builder, trading platforms, low-latency systems 3. Quant Trader - Decision-maker, runs capital, highest compensation variance 4. Risk Quant - Model validation, VaR, stress testing 5. AI/ML Quant - Fastest growing, 88% YoY hiring increase

Compensation:

Level Top Tier (Jane Street, Citadel, HRT)
New grad $300K-$500K+
Mid career (3-7yr) $550K-$950K
Senior (8+yr) $1M-$3M+
Star trader/PM $3M-$30M+

Interview Process:

Resume → Online assessment (Zetamac, logic puzzles) → Phone screen → Superday (3-5 interviews)

Jane Street specifics: Problems intentionally too hard to solve alone - test how you use hints and collaborate. 2/3+ recent intern class studied CS, 1/3+ studied math.

Three Things I Wish I'd Known

1. Estimation error is the real enemy - Full Kelly, unconstrained Markowitz, overfit ML all fail from overfitting noisy parameter estimates. Best quants respect the gap between theory and practice.

2. Tools have democratized. Conviction hasn't. - Edge lives in unique data, unique models, or unique execution - not better pip installs.

3. The math is the moat - AI can write code. But deriving why Itô's lemma has an extra term, proving martingales under risk-neutral measure - that mathematical fluency separates quants who build edge from quants who borrow it. Borrowed edge expires.
